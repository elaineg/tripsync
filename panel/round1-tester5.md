CLARITY — Yes. H1 "Turn a messy itinerary into a shared day-by-day calendar — no app, no login" plus "Add events to Google Calendar or download as .ics" told me exactly what it is and who it's for in one scroll. The two share rows are labeled perfectly: "Edit link — anyone can edit" vs "View-only link — read-only," with a tiebreaker line "Edit = companions who plan with you · View-only = anyone you just want to show." Not confusable.

VALUE — Yes, more than once. Today I'd build the itinerary in Notion and paste it into a group text or a Canva graphic, then manually re-type events so people could add them to their calendars. Here I pasted my Tahoe itinerary, it parsed 7 events across 3 days with a clean confirm-preview, and the view-only link let me share a read-only plan where each event has a one-tap "Add to Google Calendar" + a whole-trip "Download .ics." That's the exact two-step I do for every girls' weekend AND every work offsite — recurring.

VIEW-ONLY FEATURE (judged on its own) — Strong. Distinct /v/ URL, yellow "View-only — you can't edit this trip. Ask the trip owner for the edit link" banner, NO Day/Week/Month edit affordances, clicking a slot does nothing (no dead editor), and the event popup shows only "Add to Google Calendar" + "Close" with a "Proposed by the organizer" subline — no Edit/Delete/Save dead buttons. Personal add-to-Google-Calendar and .ics both work in view-only. The Edit link still fully edits (popup had Edit/Delete/Save .ics/Add to Google Calendar). Copy button flipped to "Copied ✓". The ONLY defect is the leftover edit hint on empty days.

ADVOCACY — 8. I'd recommend it for trip planning; the view-only/edit split is genuinely well done and is the thing I'd mention. Held back from 9 by: (1) the empty-day read-only copy leak above, and (2) the visual styling is competent-utilitarian, not screenshot-pretty — so it doesn't clear my "polished like my Canva board" bar. To be explicit: the view-only feature itself earns a 9; the ~1 point off is split between that small copy bug and the overall calendar aesthetic, NOT the feature's logic.
