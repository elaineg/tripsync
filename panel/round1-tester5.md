I'm Dana — demand-gen marketer, ruthless about time, I screenshot tools I like into the team channel. I planned this as a Charleston girls' weekend.

CLARITY — Yes. The H1 "Turn a messy itinerary into a shared day-by-day calendar — no app, no login" plus the subline "Add events to Google Calendar or download the whole trip as a .ics file" told me exactly what it is in under 5 seconds. Two labeled cards — "Paste an itinerary" vs "Start from a blank calendar (Drag on the grid to add events)" — made starting obvious. No confusion at all. The "no account or email required" footer is the right brag. This is the clearest part of the whole product.

VALUE — No, for MY specific job. Two things matter to me: (1) one-tap to everyone's Google Calendar and (2) it looks as polished as my Canva mood board.
- (1) WORKS, and well. "Add to Google Calendar" on each event opens a correct calendar.google.com TEMPLATE link with the right title/time/timezone (it bounced to a Google sign-in because my test browser wasn't logged in — that's Google, not the app; a logged-in friend lands on a prefilled event). The .ics ("Charleston.ics") downloads, and the share link genuinely round-tripped my event into a second clean browser with no login. The "Confirm / Proposed by Someone" + optional "What's your name?" collaboration touches are smart.
- (2) FAILS my bar. The Week view is a plain beige agenda list with pale-green pills — it reads like a stripped-down Google Calendar, not something I'd screenshot proudly into the team channel. No cover image, no per-day color or icons/emoji, no theming. For a "VISUAL trip calendar," it isn't visual. I'd still build the itinerary in Canva for looks and only use this for the calendar-export plumbing — which means double work, the exact thing it's supposed to save me.

Both start paths work. The paste parser is genuinely good — caught all 6 events, flagged "end time assumed," let me edit times before confirming. Drag-to-create on the grid is real and Google-Calendar-like (drag = span, single click = 1h block, clean title+time popover). Solid mechanics.

ADVOCACY — 5. The plumbing (no-login share, Google Calendar one-tap, .ics, paste parser, drag grid) is legitimately useful and friction-free, and I'd happily tell a friend "use this to get the brunch reservation into everyone's calendar." But I would NOT bring it up unprompted, because it doesn't deliver the "polished/visual" promise its own headline makes — and visual polish is half of why I'd reach for it over a shared Google Cal I already have. A polite 7 would be lying; it's a real 5 until it looks like something I'd screenshot.
