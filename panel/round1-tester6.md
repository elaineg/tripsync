CLARITY — Yes. Headline "Turn a messy itinerary into a shared day-by-day calendar — no app, no login" plus the footer "No account or email required" told me exactly what it is in 5 seconds. As a marketer who lives by no-login share links, this is precisely my pitch to friends. Two start cards (Paste an itinerary / Start blank) made the entry obvious.

VALUE — Yes, more than once. Today I'd drop the plan in a Notion page or a group-chat message and people lose it. This gives me a single link, no signup, that lands my friends on the actual trip dates (Aug 7-9 auto-selected, not "today" — nice) and lets them push it to their own Google Calendar. I'd reuse it for every group trip, dinner, and meetup.

VIEW-ONLY (the new bit) — clearly works. Two links are right there in the share bar and unmistakably distinct: "Edit link — anyone can edit" (Copy invite link) vs "View-only link — read-only" (Copy), with the perfect helper line "Edit = companions who plan with you · View = anyone you just want to show." That maps 1:1 onto my camping crew: collaborators get edit, the "just show me when we leave Friday" people get view. Opened the view link in a fresh tab: yellow banner "View-only — you can't edit this trip…", events render muted/dashed, NO add/create/edit controls, no dead buttons. Clicking an event opens a read-only card ("Proposed by the organizer" + "Add to Google Calendar" + Close) — no Save/Delete. Personal export ("Download .ics" / Add to Google Calendar) still works in view-only, which is exactly right. Edit link still fully edits ("Saved").

WHY 8, NOT 9-10 — Two snags.
