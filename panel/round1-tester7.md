CLARITY — Yes. The hero "Turn a messy itinerary into a shared day-by-day calendar — no app, no login" plus the two cards (Paste an itinerary / Start from a blank calendar) told me exactly what this is in ~10s: a no-login way to turn my ugly Notion bullet plan into a real shared calendar. As a designer the landing is restrained and legible; nothing fought me.

VALUE — Yes, and more than once. Today I keep trip plans as a bulleted Notion page that looks rough on my phone; copying to a real calendar means hand-entering every event. Pasting my Lisbon list and getting a "Preview parsed events — 4 events across 2 days, end time assumed (1h)" confirm step, then a real day-grid, is a genuine time-save I'd reach for every trip. The parse-then-confirm UX is the standout craft: it resolved "Oct 3 → Sat, Oct 3" and let me fix times before committing.

VIEW-ONLY CRAFT — Mostly excellent. /v/ vs /t/ routes are cleanly separated; the read-only banner ("View-only — you can't edit this trip. Ask the trip owner for the edit link to make changes.") is well-worded, calm, and placed right under the toolbar. In view-only the chrome is genuinely HIDDEN not greyed-dead: no Create New, no Your name, no Paste, no add controls — just Day/Week/Month, a "Download .ics" link, and the banner. Personal export (.ics) survives, events render as warm sand cards with title+time, and clicking an event opens nothing editable. Edit link stays fully editable (Create New, Paste, no banner). The ONE blemish is the leftover "Tap a slot to add…" hint on empty days — it's the exact dead-affordance the read-only mode otherwise avoids, and it cheapens an otherwise considered feature.

ADVOCACY — 8. I'd recommend it to friends planning trips; the two-link share model and the parse-confirm flow are better than my Notion habit. Held back from 9 by the dead empty-state edit hint in read-only, the non-parallel "Copy" vs "Copy invite link" button copy, and today-default-date making a shared link look empty on open. Fix those three and it's a 9.
